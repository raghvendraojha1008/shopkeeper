Before implementation, re-review the entire Staff Module design and validate every assumption against accounting correctness, existing ShopLedger architecture, offline-first behavior, and future scalability.

Do not proceed with implementation until all assumptions have been challenged.

Specifically review:

1. Salary Accounting

* Verify whether SALARY_PAYMENT should affect "Money With Staff" balance.
* Provide accounting justification.
* Explain alternative approaches and consequences.

2. Existing Record Linking

* Determine whether name-based linking is acceptable.
* Propose a permanent staffId migration strategy.
* Eliminate future dependence on name matching.

3. Staff Rename Safety

* Staff name changes must never break relationships.
* Staff history must remain intact.
* Staff merges, duplicates, and renames must be handled safely.

4. Balance Calculation

* Produce real-world examples with numbers.
* Verify formulas using:

  * advances
  * collections
  * expenses
  * settlements
  * reimbursements
  * salaries

5. Offline-First Compatibility

* Verify every operation works offline.
* Verify queue recovery.
* Verify no duplicate records after reconnect.

6. Firestore Optimization

* Estimate read/write impact.
* Ensure no unnecessary listeners.
* Ensure no unnecessary refetches.

7. Multi-Business Scalability

* Validate architecture for:

  * 100 users
  * 1,000 users
  * 10,000 users

8. Data Integrity

* Verify there is no possible scenario where:

  * money disappears
  * balances become inconsistent
  * transactions become orphaned
  * staff records become duplicated

9. Future Features
   Validate compatibility with:

* attendance
* commissions
* incentives
* leave management
* employee login
* branch-wise staff assignment

10. Final Architecture Review

Act as:

* Senior Accounting Software Architect
* Senior Database Architect
* Senior Product Architect
* Senior QA Engineer

Challenge every design decision.

Do not assume the current design is correct.

Identify:

* design flaws
* accounting flaws
* scalability risks
* migration risks
* future maintenance risks

Then provide:

1. Approved Design Decisions
2. Rejected Design Decisions
3. Required Changes
4. Recommended Improvements
5. Final Production Architecture

Only after this review is complete should implementation begin.
